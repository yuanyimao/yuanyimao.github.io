About me

Last updated on Thursday, December 12th 2019 at 15:14

I am a master student in the Department of Media and Communication at Shanghai Jiao Tong University. My research interests are broadly in the area of media psychology, with a specific focus on how an individual’s psychological processes, other contextual factors, and exposure to media narratives shape online pro-social behavior. At the same time, I am interested in how an individual’s potential cognitive and emotional mechanisms work. Another topic that I am interested in how people’s interactive behaviors influence their online social reading experiences. I use quantitative methods (online survey; lab experiments) and analysis (SEM and HLM) combined with qualitative methods (focus group interview).

I was born in Wuhan, and I received my B.A. in broadcasting and television hosting art from Huazhong University of Science and Technology. As I became interested in communication phenomenon (Internet and social media) and uses’ psychological effects, I choose communication science as my master major. In 2018, my paper was named Top Students Paper Award from the 2018 ICA–SJTU New Media International Conference.

I occasionally post my reading notes and some thoughts about my research field on the websites (see details in “Posts”). Also, I created an awesome list of media psychology on github and very welcome every to make contributions.
