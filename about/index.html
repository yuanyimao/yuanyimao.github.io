About me

Last updated on Sunday, February 9th 2020 at 23:50

I am a master’s student in the School of Media and Communication at Shanghai Jiao Tong University. My research interests are broadly in the area of media psychology. Specifically, I am interested in how new media (i.e., social media, mobile media, VR, etc.), in relation to situational and personal factors, impact adolescent online pro-social and deviant behavior, and considering the moral cognitive mechanisms behind this process. I use quantitative methods (online survey; lab experiments) and analysis (SEM) combined with qualitative methods (focus group interview).

I was born in Wuhan, and I received my B.A. in broadcasting and hosting art from the School of Journalism and Information Communication, Huazhong University of Science and Technology. As I became interested in communication phenomenon (Internet and social media) and uses’ psychological effects, I choose journalism and communication as my master major. In 2018, I won the Top Students Paper Award from the 2018 ICA–SJTU New Media International Forums. In 2020, one of paper that I collaborated with my supervisor has been accepted by the 70th 2020 Annual ICA Conference.

I love listening to music and tennis. I am a cat person. I also love observing and analyzing surrounding, which enables me to formulate some interesting research questions. I would put down all my personal thoughts and reading notes on my website (see details in “Blog Posts“).
